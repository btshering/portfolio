A senior U.S. defense official said Friday U.S. officials were seeing signs that Iranian fighters were engaged in Iraq, a development the source described as "concerning." But it was not immediately clear how many troops were involved.

The defense official told Fox News the United States had been watching the swift gains made by the Sunni group known as Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant (ISIS) for months, adding "this didn't just happen." "This is bad," the official said, adding that "there is not much we can do (militarily).

We are way behind the curve." The official said people in the U.S. Embassy feel threatened, but it's too "politically sensitive" to evacuate them right now.
